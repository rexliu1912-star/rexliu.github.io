On November 17, 2023, OpenAI's board abruptly fired CEO Sam Altman, citing a loss of confidence in his candor. The move triggered immediate chaos: President Greg Brockman resigned, hundreds of employees threatened to follow Altman to Microsoft, and investors were blindsided. After four days of intense negotiations and near-collapse of the company, Altman was reinstated as CEO on November 21 with a reconstituted board. The saga exposed deep tensions between OpenAI's nonprofit governance structure and its commercial ambitions, accelerating the company's push toward a for-profit restructuring.
