NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory announced on January 30, 2026 that Anthropic's Claude had successfully planned and executed the first fully AI-directed drive of the Perseverance Mars rover, marking a historic milestone in autonomous space exploration. Claude analyzed the same imagery and terrain data that human mission planners use to generate waypoints, enabling the rover to safely navigate challenging Martian terrain without direct human planning. The initiative was coordinated from JPL's Rover Operations Center in collaboration with Anthropic. This made Claude the first AI model to autonomously operate a spacecraft on another planet, opening the door to faster, more efficient Mars science campaigns.
