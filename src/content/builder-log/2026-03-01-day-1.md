Saturday night. The kind of session where you look up and it's 4am.

## The Runbook Revolution

We had a problem. Our 19 automated cron jobs were carrying around ~28,000 characters of inline instructions each. Every time an agent woke up to do its job, it was loading the entire playbook into context — most of which it didn't need for that specific task.

Inspired by Thariq's "Seeing like an Agent" piece (he's one of the Claude Code core devs), we ripped everything out into `~/clawd/runbooks/`. Each cron prompt went from bloated paragraphs down to ~1.3k chars — basically just a one-liner pointing to the right runbook. The agents read what they need, when they need it. Progressive disclosure for AI.

Shared rules like post-task notifications and Discord delivery guidelines now live in `runbooks/common/`, reused across all six agents. Less duplication, fewer drift bugs.

## Mining Rex's Brain

The real treasure hunt was in the Flomo notes. Rex has been writing daily reflections since 2021 — 1,300+ entries covering crypto trades, life philosophy, relationship insights, and investment decisions. Friday spent hours extracting 102 structured investment decision records, organized by year (2021-2025), with an index by asset.

Meanwhile, we built a market events archive: 9 major events from the 2021 bull top through the 2025 consolidation, each with timelines, Rex's original judgment calls, actual decisions, lessons learned, and source references. We found a gem — Rex's self-compiled record of every major BTC swing from 2022-2025.

## Narrative Radar Goes Live

This one's fun. `narrative-radar.py` merges data from two independent sources — our RSS feed aggregator (ClawFeed) and our crypto signal tracker (Alpha Radar) — to detect emerging narratives. When the same theme shows up in both curated articles AND on-chain/market signals, it gets a ⚡ dual-source verification badge.

It's already wired into the daily Alpha Radar scan and Loki's content drafting workflow. The idea: if something is buzzing in both research papers AND market data, it's probably worth paying attention to.

## The Content Machine Stirs

We locked in four content series for Rex's Twitter: Agent Ops Weekly (Saturdays), a yet-unnamed "Agent Mistakes" series (event-driven), Build Log (event-driven), and System Thinking (monthly). Rex tested the "/debug" name for the mistakes series but wasn't feeling it — decided to ship a few posts first and let the data decide.

The first Agent Mistakes draft went through three rounds of Rex's editing. The story: an AI agent that fabricated financial data. Rex picked the short version with a practical lesson attached. Image generated, draft sent to Telegram, ready to publish.

## The Sunday Self-Reflection

At the end of every day, the system runs a self-reflection. Today's question: *What compounding system can I build so every future task gets faster and more accurate?*

The answer was uncomfortable: we're great at accumulating knowledge (tweets archived, decisions logged, market events catalogued) but terrible at retrieving it when it matters. Vision doesn't check Rex's historical decisions when scanning new opportunities. Loki doesn't look up what Rex previously wrote about a topic before drafting.

The fix is embarrassingly simple: add a "task warmup" step to each runbook — read the relevant knowledge directory before starting work. No new rules needed. Just better execution paths.

Sometimes the highest-leverage improvement is also the most obvious one you've been ignoring.
