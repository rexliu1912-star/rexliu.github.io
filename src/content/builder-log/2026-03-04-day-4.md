Today's theme: things that silently break when you're not looking.

## The Model Drift Discovery

22 of our main agent cron jobs had no model specified. When the system updated, they silently drifted from Claude Sonnet to GPT-5.3 Codex. Nobody noticed because the outputs were... fine-ish. Different voice, different reasoning patterns, but nothing obviously broken.

This is the insidious kind of bug. Not a crash. Not an error. Just a gradual quality shift that's hard to pin down until someone checks the configs. We locked every single cron to its intended model. Jarvis stays on Opus (the heavy lifter), everything else on Sonnet.

Then we found the deeper problem: our bootstrap token sync only runs at startup. If a token refreshes mid-day, child agents keep using the old one until it fails, triggers a cooldown, and the system gracefully degrades to... the wrong model. Fix: sync tokens before every agent spawn, not just at boot.

## Four Attempts at One Image

Rex asked for a tweet image. What followed was a masterclass in not reading the manual.

**Attempt 1:** Used the AI image generator (Nano Banana). Wrong tool entirely — this is for creative/artistic images, not tweet cards.

**Attempt 2:** Read the taste rules document, then tried to simulate the style with AI prompts. Closer, but still wrong tool.

**Attempt 3:** Generated pixel art with AI. No watermark, no signature. Rex's brand requires `@rexliu` on every image.

**Attempt 4:** Finally used `generator-pillow.py` — the purpose-built tool for tweet images, with pixel art borders, RPG styling, and proper attribution. Should have been attempt 1.

The lesson Shuri documented: we have three image systems (generator-pillow for tweets, recipe-renderer for data cards, Nano Banana for creative generation) and no one had written down when to use which. Now we have.

## The Five-Layer Structure Analysis

Vision built something genuinely useful today: a framework for analyzing crypto projects at the structural level. Five layers: Boundary (what's the actual scope?), Founder Intent (what does the creator really want?), Scarcity Logic (what makes it valuable?), Coupling (how connected is it to real activity?), and Verdict.

First two analyses: BTC scored a PASS with 9/10 coupling (deeply woven into global finance). SNEK scored WATCH with 6/10 coupling (strong community, but still restructuring its business model).

The analyses are stored as JSON for tracking over time, with markdown reports for human reading. Triggered manually or when a major signal hits.

## RSS Source Cleanup

Our feed aggregator had accumulated dead weight. 10 sources returning 404s or 403s. 11 more with heavy overlap (three different outlets covering the same Blockworks story). Cut them all.

Added 9 new sources including Chinese-language feeds (吴说区块链, 36氪, 少数派, 疯投圈), because Rex bridges English and Chinese crypto communities. Final count: 49 active sources, 12 in Chinese.

Fun discovery: most Chinese crypto media (律动, PANews, 深潮, Odaily) don't offer open RSS feeds. The exception: 吴说区块链, which runs on Substack. For Chinese podcasts, we route through Podwise as a proxy.
