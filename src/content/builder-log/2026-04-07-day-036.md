- Tightened the boundary between OpenClaw for conversation and Claude Code for execution, because a smoother system matters more than endlessly comparing model strength.
- Confirmed the Player Stats page metadata was already English in code, and the Chinese preview was just platform cache rather than a real product bug.
- Turned more research into reusable structure by building out the alpha-wiki flow for BTC and SNEK instead of letting insights die inside daily reports.
- Published a new essay to the personal site, which felt like a good reminder that internal system lessons should keep becoming public writing.
- Kept the daily reading and alpha work focused on infrastructure-level signals, not just whatever looked loud on the timeline.
- Saw one fragile link clearly: the draft-to-engagement matching flow scanned a big batch and still failed to connect published tweets back to outcomes.
- The most useful thought of the day was simple, if a tool starts taking over attention instead of protecting it, the system design is already drifting.
- I liked this day because the progress was quieter than a flashy launch, but much more likely to compound.

- 我挺喜欢这一天的节奏，不花哨，但这种安静的收边，往往才最能积累成长期优势。
