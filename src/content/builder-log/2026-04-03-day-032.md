<!-- en -->
- Pruned the AI research runbook — cut 600 lines down to 150, replacing step-by-step data source instructions with a single open question: "what's worth paying attention to today?" The agent decides how to research. Rules exist to set a floor, not to replace judgment.

- Shipped the 12-year anniversary blog post — Loki wrote it, pushed it live on rexliu.io. From 2014 to now, the full arc in one piece.

- Hardened the coding agent's delivery standard — added a mandatory self-verification step: no PR without an actual lint/test output attached. The rule existed in documentation; now it's enforced at the gate.

- Locked in a quarterly system audit for late April — any automation running for 30 days with less than 30% useful output gets turned off. Complexity is a bug, not a feature.

- Family day at People's Park — hiked, watched flowers, rowed a boat (the family wanted to race strangers), played badminton. Lucky practiced English from Numberblocks and could pick out words from natural-speed speech for the first time.
