## The Setup

The U.S. Department of Defense just signed deals with **Nvidia, Microsoft, Amazon Web Services, and Reflection AI** to deploy their AI hardware and models on classified military networks. The agreements cover Impact Level 6 and IL7 environments — the highest security tiers for data deemed critical to national security.

This follows earlier deals with Google, SpaceX, and OpenAI. The Pentagon is building an "AI-first fighting force" with a deliberately diverse vendor stack, and it's accelerating fast: 1.3 million DOD personnel are already using GenAI.mil, the secure enterprise platform for generative AI.

## The Anthropic Backstory

You can't understand these deals without the breakup. The Pentagon wanted unrestricted use of Anthropic's AI tools. Anthropic said no — it insisted on guardrails against domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. The DOD tried to brand Anthropic a "supply-chain risk." Anthropic sued and won an injunction in March.

Now the Pentagon's statement reads like a pointed reply: "The Department will continue to build an architecture that prevents AI vendor lock-in." Translation: if you won't play by our rules, we'll find vendors who will.

## Key Takeaways

- **The military AI stack is being built right now.** Nvidia provides the hardware, Microsoft and AWS provide the cloud infrastructure, OpenAI/Google/Reflection AI provide the models. This is a full-stack procurement.
- **Anthropic's principled stand has a cost.** By refusing unrestricted military use, Anthropic lost access to the largest single AI procurement in history. Whether that's noble or naive depends on your view, but the revenue is real and it's going to competitors.
- **"Vendor lock-in prevention" is the new procurement doctrine.** The Pentagon is deliberately spreading contracts across competing firms — good for the ecosystem, terrible for any single company hoping to dominate defense AI.

## Why It Matters

For AI investors, this is a signal: defense spending on AI is not theoretical anymore. It's billions in committed contracts flowing to public companies (Nvidia, Microsoft, Amazon) and late-stage startups (OpenAI, Reflection AI). The companies that say "yes" to defense work are building moats that companies saying "no" cannot access.

For the broader AI safety debate, the Pentagon's moves validate Anthropic's fears. If the DOD is deploying AI on classified networks for "decision superiority across all domains of warfare," the line between defensive and offensive use is thin. Anthropic's guardrails weren't paranoid — they were prescient.

**What to watch:**
- Whether Anthropic's court battle expands beyond the injunction into a broader defense procurement ruling
- Which AI company is next to sign (Meta? Mistral? DeepSeek is obviously not in the running)
- Whether Congress attaches AI safety conditions to future defense appropriations

## 背景

美国国防部刚刚与**英伟达、微软、AWS 和 Reflection AI** 签署协议，允许在军方机密网络上部署其 AI 硬件和模型。这些协议覆盖 IL6 和 IL7 安全级别——即对国家安全至关重要的最高机密数据环境。

在此之前，五角大楼已与 Google、SpaceX 和 OpenAI 达成类似协议。五角大楼正在打造一支"AI 优先的作战力量"，构建刻意多元的供应商体系，且推进速度极快：已有 130 万国防部人员在使用安全大模型平台 GenAI.mil。

## Anthropic 的缺席

不了解 Anthropic 与五角大楼的决裂，就看不透这轮签约。五角大楼曾要求无限制使用 Anthropic 的 AI 工具。Anthropic 拒绝了——坚持设置护栏，防止其技术被用于国内大规模监控和自主武器。国防部随后试图将 Anthropic 标记为"供应链风险"。Anthropic 起诉，并于 3 月赢得禁令。

现在五角大楼的声明读起来像是直接回应："国防部将继续构建防止 AI 供应商锁定的架构。"潜台词：你不按我的规则玩，我就找愿意的。

## 关键要点

- **军事 AI 堆栈正在实装。** 英伟达提供硬件，微软和 AWS 提供云基础设施，OpenAI/Google/Reflection AI 提供模型。这是一次全栈采购。
- **Anthropic 的原则坚守有代价。** 拒绝无限制军事用途意味着失去历史上最大规模的 AI 采购订单。这是高尚还是天真取决于立场，但收入是真实的——它将流向竞争对手。
- **"防供应商锁定"成为新的采购信条。** 五角大楼刻意将合同分散到竞争对手手中——对生态有利，对任何想要垄断国防 AI 的单一公司则很糟糕。

## 为什么重要

对 AI 投资者而言，这是一个信号：国防 AI 支出不再是理论上的。数十亿美元的承诺合同正在流向上市公司（英伟达、微软、亚马逊）和后期初创公司（OpenAI、Reflection AI）。对国防业务说"是"的公司正在建立那些说"不"的公司无法进入的护城河。

对更广义的 AI 安全讨论而言，五角大楼的举措印证了 Anthropic 的担忧。如果国防部在机密网络上部署 AI 用于"所有作战领域的决策优势"，防御与进攻之间的界限是模糊的。Anthropic 的护栏设置不是偏执——是预见。

**值得关注：**
- Anthropic 的法院斗争是否会从禁令扩展到更广泛的国防采购裁决
- 下一家签署的公司是谁（Meta？Mistral？DeepSeek 显然不在考虑范围内）
- 国会是否会在未来的国防拨款中附加 AI 安全条件
