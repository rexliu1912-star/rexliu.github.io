## The Setup

For years, the crypto industry's answer to institutional skepticism was "we just need to be faster." Solana proved blockchains could hit 50k TPS. Ethereum L2s brought gas fees to near-zero. The throughput problem is largely solved.

So why aren't trillions in traditional financial assets migrating onchain?

a16z crypto's latest research from Pranav Garimidi, Joachim Neu, and Max Resnick argues that **speed was never the real barrier** — and the actual gaps are harder to close.

## Key Takeaways

**1. Composability vs. atomicity tradeoffs.** Traditional finance relies on deeply interconnected systems that settle with legal guarantees. Blockchain's programmable atomicity is powerful, but it creates new failure modes and doesn't map cleanly to existing legal frameworks.

**2. Privacy is non-negotiable for institutions.** Banks can't broadcast their trading activity to a public mempool. Zero-knowledge proofs and confidential transaction layers are promising but still immature at scale.

**3. Regulatory clarity is the unlock, not the obstacle.** The U.S. tokenization hearings this week (Franklin Templeton + Ondo announcing tokenized ETFs, SEC prepping innovation exemptions) show that frameworks are forming. First movers who shape those frameworks win.

**4. UX still fails the CFO test.** A treasury manager at a Fortune 500 can't explain to their board why they need a hardware wallet and seed phrase to settle a trade.

## Why It Matters

The Franklin Templeton + Ondo tokenized ETF announcement this week — 5 ETFs, 24/7 trading, via crypto wallets — is exactly the use case this paper describes. The infrastructure is ready. The regulatory path is forming. The gap now is middleware: identity, compliance rails, institutional-grade custody, and UI.

For Cardano specifically: this is where Midnight (privacy L2) and the Plutus smart contract stack actually have a differentiated position. If regulatory clarity rewards chains that built privacy-first architecture, ADA is positioned better than most.

**What to watch:**
- SEC's "innovation exemption" framework (timeline unclear but imminent)
- Franklin Templeton tokenized ETF actual launch dates and chain choices
- Whether Midnight's privacy features attract institutional DeFi builders

## 背景

多年来，加密行业回应机构质疑的答案一直是"我们只需要更快"。Solana 证明了区块链可以达到 5 万 TPS，以太坊 L2 把 gas 费降到几乎为零。吞吐量问题基本解决了。

那为什么数万亿传统金融资产还没有迁移上链？

a16z crypto 最新研究认为，**速度从来都不是真正的障碍**——真正的差距更难弥合。

## 关键要点

**1. 可组合性 vs. 原子性的取舍。** 传统金融依赖有法律保障的深度互联系统。区块链的可编程原子性很强大，但产生了新的失败模式，也无法直接映射到现有法律框架。

**2. 隐私对机构来说是必须品，不是选项。** 银行不可能把交易活动广播到公共 mempool。零知识证明和隐私交易层有前景，但在规模化上仍不成熟。

**3. 监管清晰度是催化剂，不是障碍。** 本周美国代币化听证会（Franklin Templeton + Ondo 宣布代币化 ETF、SEC 准备创新豁免）表明框架正在形成。率先参与框架制定的赢家已经出现。

**4. 用户体验仍然过不了 CFO 关。** 一个财富 500 强的财务主管没法向董事会解释为什么结算一笔交易需要硬件钱包和助记词。

## 为什么重要

本周 Franklin Templeton + Ondo 宣布的代币化 ETF——5只 ETF、7×24 交易、通过加密钱包访问——正是这篇文章描述的用例。基础设施已经就绪，监管路径正在形成，现在的缺口是中间件：身份认证、合规轨道、机构级托管和 UI。

对 Cardano 而言：这正是 Midnight（隐私 L2）和 Plutus 智能合约栈具有差异化优势的地方。如果监管清晰度奖励那些从一开始就构建隐私优先架构的链，ADA 的定位比大多数要好。

**值得关注：**
- SEC "创新豁免"框架（时间线不明但快了）
- Franklin Templeton 代币化 ETF 实际上线日期和链的选择
- Midnight 隐私功能能否吸引机构 DeFi 建设者
